Donor Eligibility and Precautions

Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible to donate blood, individuals must meet the following criteria:

  • Be at least 18 years old (age requirements may vary by region).
  • Weigh at least 110 pounds (50 kg).
  • Be in good general health and feeling well on the day of donation.
  • Have a valid photo ID, such as a driver's license or passport.
  • Meet specific health and travel-related eligibility criteria, including recent international travel and medical conditions. Please consult with our staff for detailed information.

Precautions for Donors

Donors should take the following precautions before and after donating blood:

  • Get a good night's sleep before the donation.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced meal at least 3 hours before the donation, and drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.
  • Avoid alcohol consumption for at least 24 hours before donating blood.
  • Avoid strenuous physical activities for the rest of the day after donation.
  • If you experience any unusual symptoms after donation, such as dizziness or nausea, inform the staff immediately.

Your safety and well-being are our top priorities, and we appreciate your willingness to contribute to this life-saving cause. If you have any concerns or questions about eligibility or precautions, please don't hesitate to contact our team for guidance.
